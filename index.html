Hull has a strong folk tradition

It's status as a major port over the centuries meant that ideas and influences from different cultures and nationalities have been readily assimilated, further adding to the richness and diversity of those traditions.

The city also played an important part in reviving and preserving the musical heritage of The British Isles during the post-war folk revival, with the establishment of The Folk Union One Club above Ye Old Blue Bell pub in 1963 by The Watersons, who would go on to forge one of the most influential family dynasties in British folk music.

Minerva Beer Festival Information

The perfect accompaniment to the weekends music and sessions is provided by the Minerva beer Festival, offering a wide selection of beers wines and spirits from local and international brewers including:


Yorkshire Brewing Co., Atom, Great Newsome, Brass Castle, Great Heck, Revolutions and more. Plus real ciders from Lilley's, Westons and Thatchers.

Forward to 2017 and the City of Culture

Hull was announced the winner of UK City of Culture 2017 in 2013. The award is given every four years to a city that demonstrates the belief in the transformational power of culture.
